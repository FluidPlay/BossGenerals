Boss Generals for Command and Conquer™ Generals Zero Hour is a massive mod project that was originally developed along two years and released in 2005. Due to its scope, it is better described as an "expansion pack mod"— featuring 15 revamped maps from the best available in the community, two new "Boss songs" for each faction (including an amazing intro score created by and authorized by C&C composing legend Frank Klepacki), tons of new abilities, and entirely new tech buildings.

Following the success of Command & Conquer Generals, Electronic Arts released the Zero Hour expansion, featuring 9 Generals based on the initial three factions: USA, China, and the terrorist organization known as GLA (Global Liberation Army). These Generals specialized in specific areas, such as infantry, air, or tank units. Gameplay-wise, Boss Generals works as a "specialty generals blender"—reuniting these specialized styles into 3 "Boss Generals" who must earn General Points in battle to unlock special units and powers until achieving the ultimate dream army: the Boss Army. This is achieved through the innovative Genplan system - instead of granting full power from the start, players the initial General Point to define their starting army style, adapting and improving their forces as the match progresses to counter their opponent's strategy. Unlike conservative approaches in other games, that might only grant a single bonus unit, the Genplan system redefines the faction on a deep level, trading away certain basic units in exchange for the specialized power chosen.

Using the USA as an example, you can focus on heavy air power by picking the King Raptor Genplan, or opt for a strong ground start with the Laser Paladin Genplan. If you choose the King Raptor, you won't have access to tanks and vice-versa. It's a tough choice, and what makes it truly interesting is that your opponent cannot know which Genplan you picked initially. This adds a layer of complexity and excitement to team games, vastly increasing replayability.

This is the biggest gameplay innovation in Boss Generals — never before in an RTS have you played something so similar to a game of cards. You don't know which "card" your opponent has played, and failing to pick a card yourself will hinder your progress. If you pick a card that your opponent can easily beat, you might not have time to recover. That's where the excitement begins — but it doesn't stop there. As you earn points in battle, you can mix Genplans to create powerful combos, such as Stealth-Toxin Tunnel Networks, Laser Paladin Tanks with King Raptor air support, or Nuclear Tanks with Attack Outposts. Instead of being limited to only 9 Generals, you have 21 flexible combinations to tailor your own army.

The counter system has also been refined in Boss Generals. For example, tank fire is enhanced against anti-infantry vehicles, which in turn deal less damage to tanks. This emphasizes unit mixing and micro-management skills, discouraging "unit spamming" and rewarding tactical players, who gets the upper hand in every engagement.

Another major highlight are the new Boss Hero Powers, available to high-level Generals. These powers complement the faction's profile: the US hero, Col. Burton, gains a new E-Shock Grenade launcher capable of inflicting a massive EMP shock that can send even the heaviest vehicles flying. The GLA hero, Jarmen Kell, uses the "Demoralizer"— a long-range flare launcher that marks a location for a plane to drop enemy prisoners (alive, but without parachutes). This horrifying display severely deteriorates enemy performance for a time, perfect for breaking through heavy defenses. China's Black Lotus is deadlier than ever with her new 'Seduce' ability, allowing her to hijack enemy vehicles by seducing the pilot and potentially turning the tide of war in China's favor.

During Boss Generals intense development period, we kept the project low-profile while we worked to ensure a professional level of polish. The beta-testing process has been extensive, involving select top players from the very early stages. We held weekly LAN testing sessions for months with experienced players and co-designers I had collaborated with for nearly a decade. Additionally, a massive 32-player LAN event in Hungary, hosted by Gaspara, tested the Boss 0.99 Pre-release version. The feedback was excellent — players loved the gameplay combinations and new powers, and the build was remarkably stable with no bugs or desync errors.

Among the 15 new maps, several are standouts. Boss Tournament Green is a remake of Tournament Desert, adding a more open, grassy theme to the most played C&C Generals map of all time. Fallen Empire Remix is an innovative map for 4 to 6 players, designed to suit both 2v2 and 3v3 matches with protected rear bases that encourage strategic support via air units and technology weapons.

With a refined counter system, deeper strategy, 15 new maps, and a wealth of new abilities, we are confident you will find plenty to enjoy in Boss Generals.
