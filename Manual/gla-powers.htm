- Differently from normal Generals points, that enable one power or extra unit, Boss Generals player's early strategy is defined by his General Plan - "Genplan". This first point will enable upgrades, new units and different unit and structure behaviours, totally changing the gameplay for both you, general, and your opponent - as soon as he realizes which genplan you picked, which can be too late! Be warned that, although you can save the early point to spend on a normal general point later on, refusing to use the incredible power of the genplans since the very early game may prove extremely hard and cost you games.

 


Stealth Tactics

  


For the terrorism-general that loves being sneaky and scaring his opponent with camouflage tactics, this is the genplan of choice. It enables to the sneaky general the Stealth Hijacker, the Sabotage plans upgrade, camo netting and the Toxin Marauder's decoy upgrade. Sabotage Plans enables two infantry abilities: Hijacker's become enabled to subvert an enemy structure to the terror organization, and rebels receive booby trap equipments. Camo netting is free and automatic for the stinger sites and network tunnels, and for all other structures is also cheaper, costing only $300. The Fake Command Center and Black Market are only buildable with this genplan, and can't be turned into their real counterparts - instead, they are manual-activated bombs of huge range and power. The Decoy Marauder upgrade is purchased per tank, costs $200 and lasts for 40 secs; It's a wire-controlled clone that always follows its spawner, excellent to confuse and drag enemy fire, since it only has a couple side airbags of difference to a real Toxin Mara. Although it has no real damage output, the Decoy Marauder also fakes firing to enhance the illusion effect, and is being considered by the enemies the low tech GLA response for missile evasion. The Toxin Marauder must be unlocked by the Toxin Weapons genplan.

Technical Details:
- Sabotage upgrade buildcost $900, research time 45s (barracks)
- Hijackers' Subversion only works on enemy structures, not on neutral ones, 15s capture time
- Booby Trap is oly placeable on neutral structures
- Fake Command Center and Fake Black Market are in fact super-bombs of devastating firepower
- Decoy Marauder upgrade $200 per tank, 5 secs buildtime, lasts for 40 secs
- Decoy Marauder is more resistant after Junk Repair is researched

Combos:
- 2 hijackers + sabotage upgrade + 4 RPGs + 1 technical - RPGs and technical distract opponent while HJs subvert key structures
- 2 workers + 2 hijackers + 2 technicals - Fast and deadly stealth rush. Workers build stealth tunnels ASAP, techs kill defending (anti-worker) infantry, hijackers capture incoming vehicles
- 3-4 quads with 2 hijackers to capture tanks sent for countering


Toxin Weapons

 


If Toxin Tunnels and Toxin Marauders are the toys that most turn you on, search no more! Costing slightly more than a normal tunnel ($900 vs $800), the improved Toxin Tunnels can be turned into the amazing stealth-toxin Tunnels that only Boss GLA has, with the stacking of the Stealth Tactics genplan as soon as you have enough battle experience to unlock all that power. One important thing to notice is that Toxin Tunnels do NOT require a barracks, being excellent for rushes. The Toxin Marauder comes with standard Toxin Shells, and gets a sensible reload increase with the Toxin Reloader upgrade.

Technical Details:
- Toxin Tunnel costs $900, builds in 32s (game time, vs 30 or standard tunnels)
- Toxin Tunnel doesn't require a barracks to be built
- Toxin Marauder costs $850, buildtime 16s
- Toxin Reloader upgrade at palace, cost $1000, research time 30s

Combos:
- 2 arms dealers, 2 forward tunnels, 2 ToxMaras - The dreaded 'Scrap Rush'. Objective is killing the defending tanks with the RPGs from the tunnels and picking the scraps with the Tox Maras to quickly achieve base-leveling firepower.
- 4 workers + 4 forward tunnels - Fastest GLA rush, and one of the hardest to stop. Workers build toxin tunnels on various different points and use the earned RPGs to pressure the opponent, with more tunnels being built meanwhile. Be warned: Critical cash rush.

 


Demo Tools

  


No terror, no toxins, just good'ol blow-em-away style! That's the chant of the Demo Tools GLA genplan, that makes the feared terror bike available to the player, as well as the scud launcher and the Scorpion and its demo rocket upgrade. Scuds take advantage of the MAPATs upgrade, and after picking scrap upgrades (which can be achieve by the crate drop general power), scud launcher missiles become incredibly resistant to anti-missile measures. Double-scrapped Scuds also receive a new demolition scud missile, extremely effective against structures. Demo Terror is a supply stash's $500 upgrade available only to the demolition-loving general, and adds nothing less than 35% extra power to terrorists, and a whole whooping 50% extra damage to gamma terrorists, meaning you won't wanna stop using and upgrading them as the battle progresses and opponents get more prepared. Demo Terror also improves terrorist-bike damage, and the Gamma upgrade improves it even more. The "suicide demo" upgraded bike has the highest damage, overlapping all previous damage increases - impressive 1000 hitpoints of damage, which in practice means that 2 suicide demo terrorbikes can destroy a War Factory.

Technical Details:
- Scorpion "Demo" Rocket is 15% stronger than the original, cost $1000, research time 40s
- Scud Launcher missile becomes 2x and 5x more resistant to PDL and ECM, with 1 and 2 scrap-upgrades respectively
- Demo Terror upgrade on supply stash, research cost $250, research time 30s

Combos:
- 2 arms dealers, 4 scorpion tanks, Scorpion Rocket - Traditional 'Scorpion Sting'. Scorpions are pumped continuously and quickly hit and run on multiple fronts, taking advantage of the scorpion superior speed and micromanaging the scorpion rockets to kill defending infantry.
- 2 scorpion tanks, 2 terror bikes - Idea is luring opponent into going after the scorpions, while the terror bikes kill his dozers or power plant, delaying him down. Scorpion rocket is delayed, continuous terrorbike-pumping is key.
- 2 forward tunnels and two barracks - 'Molotov rush'. Barracks continuously pump rebels which are immediately equipped with molotov cocktails, RPGs target incoming vehicles while rebels are switched between molotov and toxingun to kill enemy infantry and structures
- Demo Terror + 2 barracks + 2 technicals + 8 terrorists - Hard to execute but very effective, technicals invade at the same time from two different sides and try reaching key enemy structures, specially cash, wiping them out.

 

Camouflage
***

   This 3-star general point makes all toxin rebels invisible even while moving, and this Boss GLA version also makes stationary radar vans invisible to the enemy which's a nice addition in many situations in the battlefield. Camouflaged molotov rebels are always a pesky GLA threat for the enemy, specially in numbers.

Rebel Ambush
  ***

   The Boss GLA rebel ambush is performed by 6 toxin rebels, and as the original can be activated anywhere in the battlefield, though as usual using radarscan or some other scouting procedure in the target destination is always advisable, to avoid wasting this valuable power in well defended or mined areas. Bear in mind that the Boss Rebel Ambush damaging power can be highly increased with the purchase of molotov cocktails for the rebels to quickly kill key enemy structures.

Militia Mob
   ***

  Enables the Boss GLA improved Militia Mob, that is trained to move faster, comes with AK-47s and can even hit air threats with this machinegun.

Cash Bounty 2
   ***

   Equivalent to the original Generals level 2 cash hack, it's specially interesting in GLA vs GLA fights where the unit losses are generally vast for both armies. Returns a cash bounty of 15% of the value of all killed enemy units.

Boss Hero Power
  Demoralize
   *****

  Makes Jarmen Kell able to fire a signaling flare mortar that, when hitting the ground, marks the spot for the demoralize drop. This terrible strategy consists in inflicting fear and therefore greatly reducing enemy efficiency (rate of fire) in the battlefield, by dropping captured enemy civilians and soldiers by plane - without parachutes! The terrifying view of the innocents screaming while they fall for sure death, quickly turned into ground-smashed bleeding corpses scares and weakens the enemy forces, and is specially useful for breaking spikes (heavy defensive formations), since even a small army can easily obliterate the toughest defensive formation if it's demoralized. It can also help a lot against large armor, like what's common for the China armies.

Crate Drop
   *****

  Jackal trusts deeply in enhancing his forces with stealed enemy technology. The tech components that drops from destroyed enemy forces, once left as trash in the battlefield, are now gathered by workers after the battles and sent to the Secret Central Palace of the GLA High Command. From there, support planes are sent to drop these preserved tech crates gathered from previous battles and reinforce Boss GLA armies far away. These special drop crates take longer before disappearing, and give weapon upgrades (up to two levels depending on the unit), or if the unit is already with maximum weapon a experience upgrade (up to heroic), and if totally maxed out gives roughly $200 in cash. With a total of 8 crate boxes dropped by each support plane, it's easy to get, eg. 2 elite double-turret marauders, or 2 double-turret marauders and 2 double-scrap quads, or 4 double-armored battlebuses, which make up for excellent additions to your army. If you want to use it just for cash picking (roughly $1,600 total), use workers - which can't get weapon or veterancy upgrades - or another totally maxed out unit.

GPS Scrambler 2
*****

  This is a fast-recharge GPS scramble, that render all units inside the effect radius invisible indefinitely. Very nice to stealth Scud Launchers and Quads for surgical AA coverage/countering.

Militia Ambush
*******

   The Special Militia Mob that is specially trained to do a surprise ambush anywhere in the battlefield, although just as destructive, differs from a normal Mob in many senses. First, the units can be controlled individually, meaning you can spread their firepower across the enemy base to give even more trouble to the opponent. Second, the militia Ambush members do not self-heal. Last but not least, the Militia Ambush can be loaded in tunnels, totally or partially. Confidential: Combos of militia ambush and sneak attack tunnel used at the same time in different places of enemy emplacements has been seen wiping out entire bases.

Sneak Attack Tunnel
*******

  Just like the original one, excellent for popping a full army right in the middle of the enemy base or outpost.
