- Differently from normal Generals points, that enable one power or extra unit, Boss Generals player's early strategy is defined by his General Plan - "Genplan". This first point will enable upgrades, new units and different unit and structure behaviours, totally changing the gameplay for both you, general, and your opponent - as soon as he realizes which genplan you picked, which can be too late! Be warned that, although you can save the early point to spend on a normal general point later on, refusing to use the incredible power of the genplans since the very early game may prove extremely hard and cost you games.

 


Assault
  

The Assault genplan is the perfect opening strat for players who don't like taking chances, enabling the Laser Paladin and the Delta Engine upgrade for Tomahawks. The most technologically advanced MBT (Main Battle Tank) ever created, the 'Laser Pally' is very hard to be countered early by opponent forces due to its strong armor, attack power - in the form of a laser cannon - and the ability to destroy incoming missiles with his PDL (Point Defense Laser). Using it from the very beginning in an aggressive fashion has the side effect of leaving you open for anti-air units economy harrassment, and since Chinooks are so expensive and vulnerable, that's something to bear in mind. Laser Paladin doesn't excel the original Paladin only in raw firepower, once upgraded to composite armor it receives 50% more resistance than the old model - something repeatedly requested by Generals used to the - now obsolete - Crusader's composite endurance in the battlefield. Delta engines make Tomahawks 30% faster, and a much more versatile surgical strike weapon, able to quickly retreat when uncovered.

Technical Details:
- Laser Paladin buildcost $1,100
- Delta Engine cost $300 per Tomahawk, 30% more speed, faster turning - Composite Armor upgrade research cost $2,000, research time 60s (Strategy Center)
- Laser Cannon damage per second 15% higher than the original Paladin

Combos:
- 2 Laser Paladins + 1 avenger - Excellent vs GLA using mass RPG or Scorpion strats, beware Toxin Marauders and hijackers
- Laser Paladin + 1 flashvee - Good against China with Nuke-RPGs, flashbang rangers transported in humvees do short work on them and are protected by anti-inf by the paladin
- Laser Paladin + 1 comanche - Super versatile and powerful build, powerful ground with close-range air support

 

Shock and Awe
  

The genplan for those who like surprising and hitting from above, enabling the anti-structure Jackhammer upgrade for Guardian Sentry Drones, the Advanced Countermeasures upgrade and the world-renounmned King Raptor fighter. As much as enemy air and anti-air technology improved, the brute force and resiliance of King Raptors are almost impossible to be questioned. Unfortunately, the super-fast original PDL rate of fire couldn't be achieved after the original design project was destroyed during a failed enemy attempt of capturing it. Nevertheless, it still offers very good anti-missile protection, and the new design received a special, more bullet-resistant armor after being upgraded to countermeasures. The missile firing system has been optimized with faster missiles that although not as precise as the EP ones, rarely if ever misfire as their predecessors. The heavier missile resulted in less missiles being loaded at once on the aircraft, to keep the weight low and the performance high, but after many tests US engineering achieved a final damage output equal to the original specs, when the laser-guided missile technology is upgraded at the airfields. Advanced Countermeasures also solidly improve the resistance of Comanches and King Raptors versus anti-air threats, making them excellent options even for early game.

Technical Details:
- King Raptor buildcost $1400
- Advanced Countermeasures upgrade research cost is $600, research time 40s (Airfield), provides special armor for the King Raptor and Comanche
- JackHammer upgrade for GSDs cost $250 per drone, research time 8 secs
- LGM (Laser Guided Missiles) upgrade research cost is $1,800, research time 40s (Airfield)
- King Raptor fires 5 missiles (from 6), same damage per missile, with LGM upgrade the final damage is the same as the original

Combos:
- 2 airfields + 3 KRs + LGM upgrade - researching the LGM at one airfield and building planes in the other, for super-fast enemy main production structures (eg. warfactory) destruction
- 1 airfield + 2 KRs + 1 Comanche - Strong air-superiority build, comanche can be used to disrupt enemy tactical actions (eg. refinery capture) while the 2 KRs are built and used to destroy the incoming AA vehicle
- 1 airfield + 2 barracks + 1 KR + 6 MDs - Balanced air build, Missile defenders on both main flanks protect the airfield(s) that start massing KRs
- 2 airfield + 3 KRs + 2 EP raptors - For the micro-management gifted eagle player, the EP raptors quickly pick infantry threats while the KRs take out large enemy targets

 


Tech Devices

 

  

Tactical american generals are generally pleased to win their battles using the most technological toys available to them, getting enemies with pants down and many times with little to no time to react. If that's your description, the 'Tech Devices' genplan is the right one for you. It enables two multi-medal awarded designs of General Alexis, the EMP Patriot able to disable enemy incoming vehicles and structures - having some specially nasty aggressive usages - and the 'Power Surge' Control Rods, based on the original Advanced Control Rods and able to deliver mass amounts of power to the US base. Last but not least, allows the american General to enhance his already powerful infantry with Missile Defenders veterancy and the Speedboots upgrade, that affects missile defenders and pathfinders.

Technical Details:
- EMP Patriot cost $1,300, buildtime 30s (vs 25s of the standard patriot)
- Power Surge Control Rods - Free activation after normal control rods research at fusion power plant
- Speedboots upgrade research cost $1000, research time 45s

Combos:
- Power Surge + 2 supply centers + 2 EMP patriot drops - The 'patriot drop' which's the fastest rush tactic in the game, is incredibly maximized by the Power Surge upgrade that powers two EMP patriots being built by dozers at different spots of the enemy base
- 2 barracks + speedboots + 6 MDs - Harrassment opening. Speedboot being researched at one barracks and veteran MDs being trained at the other allows for a quickly assembled missile defender force that is able to outrun most enemy threats and kill targets of oportunity like dozers and supply trucks
- Flashbang + speedboots + 5 MDs + 2 rangers - Power-push strat. A great complimentary frontal force that can kill almost anything thrown at it early on, can be backed by a dozer intended to build a vertically progressing column of EMP patriots

 


Stealth Comanche
 ***

 

To make any existing and future comanches stealthed flying machines, all a Boss US general has to do is pick this General point and activate it for free in any existing airfield. This is a simply technology unlocking switch, meaning the upgrade has no cost and happens instantaneously.

Armored Combat Chinook
***
  

What once was limited to only some select anti-terror missions - due to its excess frailness - is now a multi-purpose flying weapon. The Combat Chinook is back, meatier and meanier in a brand new high armored version. Although still sensibly vulnerable to rocket weapons that happen to overwhelm its PDL system, the ACC resistance to anti-air weapons is highly improved, and gets even better with the composite armor upgrade.


Tactical Weapons
***

 

  

Nothing more cunning that a good strategical move impressing ang getting your opponents with pants down. For that role the Tactical Weapons general point was developed, enabling the the leaflet drop and the Tomahawk Chaining upgrade. After this 3-star-General technology is researched at the strategy center, groups of tomahawk lauchers can use the chaining link system to fire a pair of siege missiles, as soon as another nearby tomahawk "paints" the target. The leaflet drop becomes also available to the General, and drops a pack of military propaganda that dazzles and confuses the enemies, making them drop their weapons for some time - which can be used to great advantage.


Dual A-10 strike
***

  

Like the original A-10 level 2 attack, a pair of A-10s hit hard any ground target across the battlefield. Mid-fast recharge, highly effective.

 


Pathfinder
***

   Enables the training of the highest trained US Army snipers, known as "Pathfinders". Cloaking and high ranged, specially with the searh-and-destroy battleplan and/or inside garrissonable buildings, the pathfinder is able to deliver massive, generally insta-kill damage to infantry. Add to that its cloak-detection abilities and his new speedboots battle gear, and you'll quickly realize why the american elite snipers are the most respected in the world.

Aurora Alpha
*****
  Enables the building of Aurora Alphas in the Boss US airports. Costly ($2,700) and taking a long time to recharge its missile payload, the Alpha Auroras are nonetheless the most dreadful tactical air weapon of the Boss United States army. Its alpha bomb is able to create a high-powered explosion across a large area, able to destroy even massively strong vehicles that happen to cross it. Notice that, differently from the EP Aurora, Alphas do miss their targets if they're moving.


Boss Hero Power
E-Shock
*****

   Burton’s E-Shock Grenade is a powerful new tool for Col. Matt 'Laser' Burton, available as a 5-star General asset. This new high-tech weapon has been shown to shut down buildings and units while its shockwave can destroy infantry and send even the heaviest of armor flying.


Spectre Gunship
*****

   A Spectre gunship comes flying at high speed and can deploy multiple howitzers and gattling guns fire at targets hand picked in a certain area. Due to his high penetration nature, the Spectre Gunship is recommended for surgical action at the back of heavily anti-air front-guarded areas, that A-10s for example can't reach alive.

Heavy Repair
*******
   This General power repair all units across a large area, almost entirely. Due to its extreme efficiency in the battlefield, this power takes long to recharge (5 minutes) and is only available to 7-star Generals. For best results, try retreating the hurt units and moving the healthier units to the front to absorb some damage before applying the power.

Carpet Bomber
*******
  If softening multiple fronts in record time is your call, don't think twice before picking the US Airforce Carpet Bomber. Cheaper, recharging way faster than the MOAB and able to change its route to hit multiple targets in the desired area, the Carpet Bomber is a very efficient weapon in the Boss US arsenal.

M.O.A.B.
*******
   The single most powerful non-nuclear weapon developed by manking, period. Where former versions were reported as having way less damage than expected, the Boss US MOAB is being called by opponents as the secondary superweapon of the United States. The carrier Stealth Bomber is also way faster and less vulnerable to Anti-air fire than the Carpet Bomber plane, making sure the MOAB always obliterates the intended target. With all that power comes a unique price tag, the MOAB is only available to the highest experienced 7-star Generals, at a cost of two general points - and takes 6 minutes to recharge. Still, the MOAB is the perfect choice against a large clumping of opponent defenses and/or units, and there's nothing quite as morale devastating to the enemy than its superiority-imposing usage.
