Laser Ranger

One of the latest advancements in standard American technology seen on the battle field is the standard Laser Rifle which is extreamly strong when fighting infantry and has had reports of even burning troops to death. Nevertheless, the improved arsenal of Rangers do not stop there! New HP (High Powered) flashbang grenades have been issued to troops on and off the field. Tests show that with increased fire power and a longer reload time Rangers will have to micro each of their new and improved weapons for the best effect when neutralizing infantry threats.


Missile Defender

What good is such advanced weaponry when infantry lack the basic essentials? American forces are receiving new gear if the commander invest on the 'Tech Devices' genplan and is willing to pay a small price tag for the technology. Speed boots help American Missile Defenders and Pathfinders to gain a substantially increased movement rate, making them exceptional pursuing and defending units, able to outrun most of their foes. Quick response teams have been seen using hummers, speed boots and the search and destroy orders to make quick work of flanking armor support. It's worth noting that when the general picks the "Tech Devices" genplan, all missile defenders start out with veteran status, hence with a higher damage and resistance.

Pathfinders
(genplan)

The few, the proud, the elite! The Pathfinders have proven themselves time and time again in the last two campaigns against the GLA to be invaluable to the American arsenal. Pathfinders have received extra training and are also able to use the upgraded 'Speed boots' gear.

 

Col. Matt "Laser" Burton
 The Hero of the American Armed forces, whose feats have been reproduced by Holywood's silver screen more than once, is back and better than ever, with a powerful and longer ranged portable Laser cannon capable of dealing very strong armor-piercing damage to regular tanks. Burton’s E-Shock Grenade is a powerful new tool for Burton, available as a 5-star General asset. This new high-tech weapon has been shown to shut down buildings and units while its shockwave can destroy infantry and send even the heaviest of armor flying.

 


Laser Paladin Tank
(genplan)

On the forefront of new technology, the American army will be pitting the ultimate combination of Point Defense Laser support and Laser power in one respect-imposing unit. This MBT (Main Battle Tank) armored unit, albeit being responsible for rendering all the former US armor tech obsolete, is costly if a general chooses to use it, but can also be obtained by air-drop reinforcements at strategic pads found around the battlefield.


Humvee (HMMWV)

As classic and reliable as ever, the Humvee (nickname for HMMWV, a military term for "High Mobility Multipurpose Wheeled Vehicle") has been seeing some new usages in actual war situations, when coupled for example with the laser rangers rifles that deal a good deal of damage on structures and can be easily obtained from American carrier drops available as soon as the strategy center is built. Like other fast moving troop-carrying vehicles, though, the humvee is much more susceptible to tank fire recently, since latest tank shells technologies vastly improved their armor penetration attributes.

Guardian Sentry Drone (GSD)

The American armed forces are always striving for perfection of their automated troops and the latest in a long line of drones is here. Where the former versions failed, the Guardian Sentry Drone excels with a new plated armor that is gattling-fire resistant. A small long-range machinegun is now standard on all GSDs, which has the latest AI enhancements of not firing unless put into its guard mode. Field tests including a combination of Avenger and sentry drone has proven to make the unit extremely effective in numbers vs. light armored units. With a huge sight and stealth-detection range, fast movement and the new JackHammer siege attack mode - available with the Shock and Awe genplan - the GSD became a important part of the american tech tree.


Tomahawk Delta

 The long-ranged artillery vehicle from US forces is now better than ever, loaded with new siege missiles activateable with the impressive tomahawk chaining technology. After this 3-star General technology is researched at the strategy center, groups of tomahawk lauchers can use the chaining link system to fire a pair of siege missiles, super efficient against structures. To activate the system properly, just one Tomahawk should aim at the target, and the tomahawks nearby will automatically fire at it using siege missiles. When equipped with the new "Delta Engine" upgrade, available with the Assault genplan, the Tomahawk gains 30% more speed, moving swiftly across the battlefield to better position itself and retreat out of danger when uncovered.


Microwave Tank

 With a re-inforced tank-shell resistant armor plating, and with some even newer technological advances, the Microwave has been seeing increasing tactical usage by the Boss US forces. Empowered by a new and costly experimental technology, the Stealth scout drone, the Microwave is now being considered a 'natural predator' of GLA outposts.

 


EP Raptor

Replacing the former and now obsolete standard Raptor aircraft, US military designers developed an amazing flying machine - the Extra Precision Raptor, or EP Raptor for short. Cheap and fast to build, designed to satisfy the claims of the high US command which's comprised of seasoned american Generals, the EP rockets that arm the new plane not only now have an improved guidance system that makes them never miss targets - even small and moving ones like infantry - but they're also plated with a special titanium light metal compound (A-6 Titanium Aluminide alloy) that makes them double-resistant to PDL and ECM missile-defense devices.


King Raptor
(genplan)

The 'eagle' general's most loved weapon. As much as enemy air and anti-air technology improved, the brute force and resiliance of King Raptors are almost impossible to be questioned. Unfortunately, the super-fast original PDL rate of fire couldn't be achieved after the original design project was destroyed during a failed enemy attempt of capturing it. Nevertheless, it still offers very good anti-missile protection, and the new design received a special, more bullet-resistant armor after being upgraded to countermeasures. The missile firing system has been optimized with faster missiles that although not as precise as the EP ones, rarely if ever misfire as their predecessors. The heavier missile resulted in less missiles being loaded at once on the aircraft, to keep the weight low and the performance high, but after many tests US engineering achieved a final damage output equal to the original specs, after the laser-guided missile technology is upgraded at the airfields.


Stealth Fighter

The #1 anti-defense plane of the american airforce has now built-in fast PDL lasers and the armor boost of the countermeasures upgrade, making it even more efficient and with a higher return rate. Use it in pairs to quickly destroy any standard stationary defense of the enemy. Albeit not being very effective vs structures, in large maps it's smart having a good number of stealth fighters for surgical missions. The bunker buster upgrade also makes it extremely effective vs GLA, forcing units out of the tunnels and killing the weaker infantry that's hidden within.

EP Aurora Bomber

Complaints similar to the ones directed at the old Raptor model were heard about the Aurora bomber too, claiming it wasn't accurate enough to hit moving target and was therefore useless for early defense purposes. EP bombs were then projected to help alleviate this issue, and a replacement aircraft had to be designed with an advanced dropping mechanism to support the new weapon. Mixed feelings resulted from the final product, since the new EP Aurora bomber is now much more complex and therefore sensibly slower to produce, and the lighter bomb used doesn't have as high raw damage as the former one. Nevertheless the US high command felt it's a more polished and useful aircraft than the former and adopted the EP Aurora as the new standard bomber of the american forces.


Aurora Alpha Bomber
(genplan)

Highly acclaimed by the United States as the most powerful regular bomber ever created, famous General Alexis Alexander's masterpiece creation couldn't be forgotten by the new generation. As powerful and strong as ever, Aurora Alphas only negative point is that some of the key technology used to produce them became scarce due to high demand, leaping in price, and rendering the final cost of the aircraft to be much higher. Aurora Alphas take a long time to reload their missiles, so it's imperative keeping the airfield defended at all costs during the crucial reload process.


Stealth Comanche

 The Comanches were many times victims of their own machinegun range limitation, that used to bring them too close to anti-air vehicles and defenses when trying to pick individual infantry targets. Situation have improved dramatically for Boss US Comanches, that have a higher range and can now confortably be 'front-shielded' by tanks and guardian sentry drones against these threats. Albeit having the cloaking device cost incorporated to the basic helicopter cost, due to some recent 'lethal weaponry technology containment' international treaties the comanche's cloaking is only activateable by select 3-star Generals. Keep in mind that Boss comanches take longer to re-stealth than previous models.
