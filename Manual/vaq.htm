Q - First, I want to know what it is, period, and what it did in terms of modifying things. 2nd, I want to know if it is balanced. 3rd, I want to know if spam is an issue. 4th, I want to know if it is easily swapped in and out of the game...

A - Okay, one thing at a time. 1st, Boss Generals is a wholy new gameplay mode for Generals/Zero Hour, to fully understand what's it about read here, to know about the actual changes unit per unit, check here. 2nd - As far as our quality control guys and 8-month balance period could check, yes. Nothing is expected to be perfect in an initial release, but you'll most probably be surprised on how balanced it is, specially considering the complex unit combinations in the Boss genplan system. 3rd - Simply put, counters are much stronger than in the original game, so unit massing (spam) problems are only an issue in late game, and that's still questionable coz that's the moment when the uber-destruction powers kick in. 4th - yes, we have an easy loader that does it all for you.

Q - How is the game pace compared to Generals and Zero Hour?

A - Everyone who playtested it agrees that it has a "feel" and pace similar to Generals - in a more brutal fashion - with even more diversity and excitement than Zero Hour. Although the countering system is enhanced, meaning that units are killed by their counters faster, similar Units are generally made stronger to each other (like tank vs tank or artillery vs artillery) what makes micromanagement battles last satisfyingly longer.

Q - Is this going to be something of a race to the highest general level? I don't want to play something where 1st-to-hit-5-wins.

A - Boss is much more about tayloring the perfect army as the war progresses than digging points for a fast, cheap win. For you to better understand, we've fine tuned the experience gave by each army. So, for example, Boss GLA is more expendable but their units give away less experience, Boss US is the opposite. Since games generally last longer, that was essential. So US take longer to reach its uberness "Boss status" (level 7), China faster, and GLA gets points the fastest.

Q - Is it set up so that when you hit that gen level, you can suddenly rip apart half of the opponent's base with your new toys...?

A - Honestly, we've seen many more players investing *back* at basic genplans, to improve mixing, than spending on the high-level "free-kill" toys.

Q - Example: You're china. You have a big slow-ass army of tanks, and buildings with no real self defense. USA hits top level before you do, you have every ability you already had and then some new boom booms to murder everything better.

A - Okay. Strongest "boom-booms" are obviously the carpet bomber (China gets it faster, reloads faster), and the MOAB - which costs a whooping two general points and takes 6 min to recharge. How did China let US get all these points and not have theirs in the first place? ;) In our tests, only in very long-drawn games (generally teams) someone ever reach Level 7. China gets the most help from points in actual gameplay, in terms of time/efficiency, which sorts of compensate for its overall lack of mobility. US points are the best, but generally kick in at the point when they're very needed 'coz GLA spam and China combos, complimented by the almighty Boss upgrades, already exceeded US firepower on pure cash/efficiency, so US general powers work more as a balance thing. That considering similar skill levels of course, a walkover will only be made quicker by Boss general powers, sparing the victim from a too long suffering :D

Q - Does GLA have anything to break into an uber-turtled base?

A - First, the molotov cocktail upgrade for rebels. It rocks hard vs defense, although it misses too much to replace RPGs for anti-tank. Second, demoralize. It's devastating for the rate-of-fire of defenses, and the fire range is huge. Third, crate drop plus scud launchers. Very powerful. Scrapped or double-scrapped, they get increasingly resistant to PDL and ECM defensive measures. Last but not least, Militia (an improved Angry mob) ambush is very interesting. For late game, just imagine an angry mob but super powered in your base when you don't expect it. It takes long to recharge (6 min) but its units can be spread apart and even loaded into tunnels! It certainly makes up for GLA not having a MOAB or some pinky lil' plane dropping bombs.

Q - An angry mob ambush?? WTF?

A - Militia ambush is interesting, but far from overpowered. Its units can be spread apart, some of them have a short-range AA weapon and can be loaded into tunnels, which enhance their effectiveness when coupled with stealth tunnels or sneak attack tunnels. This is practically like a secondary super weapon on its own. Still, it takes long to recharge (6 min) and in any way you look at it, infantry is always very vulnerable to many counters that are plentiful at late game.

Q - I want invisible toxin rebels with the demo upgrade. Can I have'em?

A - Yeah you can have'em, but that's too shy.. how about: invisible-suicide-demo-toxin-molotov-rebels :D That's the kind of sweet you can expect from Boss Gens.

Q - How am I expected to defeat a mass of Stealthed Comanches with uber laser monsters as armor backups?

A - First, you must look at the big picture. On the ground is very hard for US handling a mass of Toxin Marauders or Nuke Battlemasters, for example, it generally needs costly air or infantry support. As boosted as the comanches are, they can't be stealthed until level 3 is reached, and by that time China already have superior detection (Super Lotus and Gattling Emperors) and detection for Boss GLA becomes very good, in the form of idle-stealthed radar vans (with the camoufrage genpoint), and the Tech Saboteur. Tech Sabs have good speed and huge line of sight and detection range. Also, Boss stealth comanches take sensibly longer to re-cloak than the original, while GLA Quads get Turbo-AP bullets upgrade (way faster firing rate) and free scrap upgrades using the crate drop gen power.

Q - Wait a moment here.. you mean you have King Raptors and Laser Paladins in the same army? Are you crazy??

A - For starters, you can't have both immediately. Each take one genplan and you have to fight effectively to reach a higher rank and mix in another genplan. At that time the opponent will have its own combo toys, like fast-reload nuke Tank Hunters and stealthed stinger sites and toxin tunnels, that makes things really complicated for the typical USA PDL abuser. You also have to consider that Laser Paladins have a slow reload rate on its turret, Avengers have a delay on its secondary PDL, and King Raptors have a slower PDL rate than the original. All these small tidbits are hard to notice separately, but they do stack up in balance's favour.

Q - Ok, roll play: I am a no-rush camper player. How do i turtle up?

A - The best bet would be going a) GLA stealth with mass stingers and tunnels + molotov cocktail rebel spam as soon as demo tools is achieved; or b) US with tech devices for mass EMP patriots, flashbang for anti-infantry then King Raptors when available.

Q - So what about China? How can a live in late game when the other sides just pwned my INF bunker camp strat with scud bugs?

A - The "scud bug" is fixed since Zero Hour's patch 1.02, and Boss Generals share these fixes. As for China late game, a large mass of fully upgraded battlemasters with some emperors or Helixes S2 for subliminal messaging rate-of-fire increase is an absolute steamroller, and in the air the fearsome Nuke-napalm Black Migs will make sure you have superior support.

Q - How is someone able to properly defend against a GLA that can be stealth or toxin? There'll be no adaptation time..

A - The worst problem vs an early game Boss GLA is when he picks Stealth Tactics. Stealth Hijackers can grab dozers (and, by quickly raising structures remotely, steal the enemy technology) and also capture buildings with the new "Infiltration" upgrade - if you're not prepared. Boss USA starts with the spy drone, a general point for the older factions, and radar scan, while Boss China have the brand new Advanced Speaker Tower, that when upgraded to detection-EMP mines have a long detection range.

Q - What's the most cost-efficient unit for each army?

A - That's really dependant on the situation and genplan picked by each army. For example, while scorpions are the most cost-effective unit early game in tank vs tank battles, due to its anti-infantry and anti-armor scorpion "demo" rocket, against USA PDL-enabled laser paladins they're not half as effective - in this case Toxin Marauders are much better.

Q - How is humvee with missile defenders (MDV) abuse handled in Boss Gens??

A - Like all other vehicles that carry troops able to fire on the move (battle bus, outpost, troopcrawler), in Boss Generals humvees are extremely vulnerable to tank fire, many times getting to red energy - therefore slowing down dramatically - with a single shot. While ground range of anti-infantry vehicles (Quad and Gattling) are improved (165 vs 150), and rate of fire improvement upgrades are available for them (Turbo chaingun and Turbo AP), the 'Search and Destroy' battleplan also has a slightly smaller effect, making MDVs risky and ineffective to use as main battle units and more adequate for harrassment and missile-support roles.

Q - Are busses g** in Boss?? (Question by Aggro)

A - Not when just-born.. you need destroyed-vehicle scraps to have them "g**" lol :D. Un-scrapped they die easily to tank fire in Boss Gens, and they're also slower than the original version.
